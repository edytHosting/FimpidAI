Chicken Care Guide

Provide a balanced diet of commercial feed, supplemented with treats and grit. Ensure fresh water is always available.

  • Chick Starter (0-8 weeks)
  • Grower Feed (8-18 weeks)
  • Layer Feed (18+ weeks)

Clean, fresh water is crucial for chicken health and egg production. Use appropriate waterers and clean them daily.

Building the Perfect Coop

Space Requirements

At least 2-3 square feet per bird inside the coop, and 8-10 square feet per bird in the run.

Ventilation

Essential for air quality and preventing respiratory issues. Ensure good airflow without drafts.

Nesting Boxes

One nesting box for every 3-4 hens is usually sufficient.

Roosting Bars

Provide sturdy roosting bars for chickens to sleep on at night, off the ground.

Common Chicken Health Issues

Mites and Lice

Symptoms: Itching, feather loss, reduced egg production. Treatment: Dusting powders, sprays, coop cleaning.

Coccidiosis

Symptoms: Bloody droppings, lethargy, ruffled feathers. Treatment: Medicated feed or water.

Always consult with a veterinarian for accurate diagnosis and treatment plans.

The Wonderful World of Chicken Eggs

Did you know an average hen lays 5-6 eggs per week?

Egg color is determined by the chicken's breed, not its nutritional value.

Storing and Washing Eggs

  • Store unwashed eggs at room temperature for a few days, or refrigerate for longer.
  • Wash eggs only right before use to preserve the natural bloom.

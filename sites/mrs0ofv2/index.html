What are cows?

Cows are large, domesticated mammals that belong to the bovine family. They are raised for their meat (beef), milk, and other dairy products. There are many different breeds of cows, each with their own unique characteristics.

Interesting facts about cows

  • Cows are social animals and form close bonds with their herd mates
  • A cow's stomach has four compartments to help digest tough plant matter
  • Cows can see almost 360 degrees around them
  • The average cow produces 6-7 gallons of milk per day
